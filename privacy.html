1. Who We Are and Who This Policy Applies To

GyandootAI is operated by GyandootAI Private Limited (company incorporation in progress), India. This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, store, protect and share your personal data when you use our service at gyandootai.com.

This policy applies to all users of gyandootai.com including teachers, educators, school administrators, and any person who submits a request or creates an account. It is compliant with India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 (DPDPA) and the DPDP Rules 2025 notified on 13 November 2025.

GyandootAI is intended for use by adults (18 years and above) who are qualified teachers or educators. It is not intended for use by students or minors.

3. How We Use Your Data — Purpose Limitation

Under DPDPA 2023, we may only use your data for the purpose for which it was collected. We use your data for:

  • Generating and delivering your question paper — primary purpose
  • Account management — tracking your plan, usage count, and subscription status
  • Service communications — confirming receipt, delivery issues, account updates
  • Support responses — when you contact us
  • Product updates — informing paid subscribers of new features (opt out anytime)
  • Service improvement — anonymised, aggregated analysis of which subjects/boards are most requested

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, selling to third parties, training AI models, or any purpose other than what is listed above.

5. Your Rights Under DPDPA 2023

Under India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023, you have the following rights as a Data Principal:

📋 Right to Access

Request a summary of the personal data we hold about you and how we use it.

✏️ Right to Correction

Ask us to correct or update any inaccurate or incomplete personal data.

🗑️ Right to Erasure

Request deletion of your personal data from our records. We will also instruct our processors to delete your data.

↩️ Right to Withdraw Consent

Withdraw your consent at any time. This will stop future processing but may mean we cannot provide the service.

📣 Right to Grievance Redressal

Raise a complaint about how we handle your data and receive a response within 7 business days.

🏛️ Right to Data Protection Board

If your complaint is not resolved, escalate to the Data Protection Board of India at dpboard.gov.in

To exercise any right: email [email protected] with the subject line "Data Rights Request" and your registered email address. We respond within 7 business days.

6. Consent — How We Obtain and Manage It

In compliance with DPDPA 2023 Section 6, we obtain your consent before collecting personal data:

  • Before submitting your paper request, you tick a checkbox confirming: "I understand this is an AI-generated practice paper, not an official board paper. I agree to the Terms of Service and Privacy Policy."
  • This constitutes free, specific, informed, and unambiguous consent as required under DPDPA.
  • You may withdraw consent at any time by emailing us. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing before withdrawal.
  • We do not use pre-ticked boxes, bundled consents, or make consent a condition of service beyond what is strictly necessary to operate it.

7. Data Security

We implement reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your data:

  • HTTPS encryption on all data transmission between your browser and our systems
  • Access controls — only authorised personnel have access to user data
  • No sensitive financial data stored — payment processing handled entirely by Razorpay
  • Third-party processors operate under their own security frameworks (Google, Anthropic, Make.com, Razorpay are all enterprise-grade secure platforms)

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to cause harm to you, we will notify you and the Data Protection Board of India as required by DPDPA, within the timeframe specified by law.

8. Browser Storage and Cookies

We use your browser's localStorage (not cookies) to track your free paper usage count on your device. This data:

  • Is stored on your device only — never transmitted to our servers
  • Contains only a numerical count linked to your email address (stored in hashed/encoded form)
  • Can be cleared at any time by clearing your browser's storage data

We do not use advertising cookies, tracking pixels, third-party analytics scripts, or behavioural profiling of any kind on our website.

9. Children's Data

GyandootAI is strictly intended for use by adults (18 years and above) who are teachers or educational professionals. We do not knowingly collect data from children or students.

  • We do not target students or minors with our service.
  • If you believe a minor has submitted personal data to us, please contact us immediately at [email protected] and we will delete it promptly.
  • Under DPDPA 2023, processing of children's data requires verifiable parental consent — we do not process children's data and have no mechanism for students to submit requests.

10. Data Retention and Deletion

  • We retain your name and email while you are an active user of the service.
  • If you have not submitted a paper in 24 months, we will delete or anonymise your personal data.
  • You may request earlier deletion at any time by emailing [email protected].
  • Anonymised, aggregated data (e.g., "X% of requests were for Class 12 Physics") may be retained indefinitely as it cannot be linked to any individual.
  • Legal hold: We may retain data for longer if required by a valid legal obligation or court order.

11. Cross-Border Data Transfer

Your data is processed by services that may operate servers outside India:

  • Anthropic (Claude AI): US-based. Your subject/topic only (no personal identifiers) is sent. Anthropic operates under SOC 2 Type II certification.
  • Make.com: EU-based. Full submission data passes through their servers. They are GDPR-compliant.
  • Google: US-based. Email delivery. Google's infrastructure meets enterprise security standards globally.

We take care to ensure these transfers are necessary, minimal, and to reputable processors. As DPDPA cross-border transfer rules are notified, we will update our practices to comply.
